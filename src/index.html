About

From History to Web Development

I have a Bachelor's degree in History from Royal Holloway and a Master's Degree in Byzantine History from the University of Oxford. Why, then, am I getting myself into something apparently completely different? Isn't programming for people who have degrees in Computer Science or Maths?

It's true! Honestly though, I've always loved working with computers. I can remember getting my first laptop and playing around with commands in the terminal, or creating awful little mods for The Elder Scrolls: Oblivion. Its just that cirmcumstances, a few books, and some really great history teachers led me to study History. I love History and I don't regret studying it. However, I wanted to apply myself to something just as challenging and creative, but also tapped into that itch I felt when I was a teenager with my first laptop.

The Journey

I started off by watching some videos and following along with some tutorials. It wasn't really for any particular end goal other than learning something new. From memory, I did some basic stuff in C#. In developer circles this is known as 'Tutorial hell', where you end up just watching someone and copying their code. Thankfully, I only did that for a few days!

I started with The Odin Project - a free and open source curriculum in Web Development. The brilliance behind The Odin Project is its ability allow learners to build independantly. In fact, it forces you to do so: there are several projects to complete and Odin only provides you with very basic guidelines - the rest is up to you to complete! This is where the real learning comes in - reading articles/documentation or following tutorials doesn't really get you anywhere. It also gives you an incredible amount of confidence. When you finish something, you can look at it and think, "I made that!"

Consolidation

Web Development and building small projects is great, but you can build a handful of these without having any understanding of how Computers actually work. This is where Harvard's CS50x comes in. This free 10 week course gave me an introduction into Computer Science. It covers a variety of topics in two languages: C, a low-level and incredibly powerful language; and Python, a high-level and very popular language. The point of CS50 is not so much to learn those languages, but to understand concepts such as Memory, Data Structures, and Recursion. I have a soft spot for C - working with pointers really makes me feel like I'm getting as close to the hardware as possible... in a digital sense, not a physical one!

Knowledge

The thing I love about Web Developement and programming (or, what I know of it so far!), is that you will never know it all. There's so much to learn, which is both daunting and exciting. It sends sparks to that part of my brain which loves to learn and be creative.
